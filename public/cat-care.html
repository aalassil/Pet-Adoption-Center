Cat Care

General instructions on how to care for a cat.

Nutrition

Provide a balanced diet appropriate for their age and health status. High-quality cat food, either dry or wet (or a combination), is essential. Fresh water should always be available.

Grooming

Regularly brush your cat to reduce shedding and prevent matting, especially for long-haired breeds. Trim their nails as needed and clean their ears and eyes gently.

Litter Box

Keep the litter box clean and accessible. Cats are clean animals and prefer a clean environment for their bathroom needs.

Exercise

Provide opportunities for play and exercise. Cats enjoy toys that mimic hunting behaviors, such as feather wands or small balls. Playtime helps keep them mentally and physically stimulated.

Veterinary Care

Schedule regular check-ups with a veterinarian and keep up with vaccinations, flea, and tick prevention. Promptly address any health concerns or behavioral changes.

Environment

Create a safe and stimulating environment at home. Cats need places to climb, scratch (provide scratching posts), hide, and rest comfortably.

Affection and Attention

Cats thrive on companionship and affection. Spend quality time with your cat daily, petting and interacting with them based on their preferences.

Monitoring Behavior

Watch for changes in behavior, appetite, litter box habits, and overall demeanor. Cats can hide signs of illness, so any unusual behavior should be checked by a vet.

Safety

Ensure your home is safe for your cat. Remove any hazards like toxic plants, chemicals, or small objects they could swallow.
